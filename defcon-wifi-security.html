üíÄWhat's Actually Happening

Here's the technical reality you should know:

  • Evil Twins - Fake APs with stronger signals than legit ones
  • Karma Attacks - Your device is broadcasting its WiFi history
  • SSL Strip - HTTPS getting downgraded to HTTP
  • DNS Spoofing - Traffic redirected to attacker-controlled servers
  • Packet Injection - Malicious code inserted into your browsing session
  • MITM Proxies - Someone reading your traffic like the morning paper

üõ°Ô∏èHow Not to Get Rekt

Defensive measures that actually matter:

  • VPN Everything - Use a VPN, preferably your own WireGuard setup
  • Disable WiFi Auto-Connect - Your phone loves to overshare
  • Use Cellular Data - 4G/5G is significantly harder to intercept
  • HTTPS Everywhere - Force SSL on all connections
  • Certificate Pinning - Don't trust random certificates
  • Tor Browser - For the truly paranoid (you know who you are)
  • Burner Devices - Bring a laptop you don't mind getting compromised
  • Faraday Bags - When you need to go completely dark

Pro Tip: If you're doing anything sensitive, maybe don't do it at DefCon.

üéØAdvanced Techniques

Next-level attacks you should be aware of:

  • KRACK Attacks - WPA2 key reinstallation vulnerabilities
  • Dragonblood - WPA3 downgrade attacks
  • PMKID Attacks - Clientless WPA/WPA2 cracking
  • Beacon Flooding - Fake AP spam to cause chaos
  • Deauth Storms - Kicking everyone off networks
  • Captive Portal Bypass - Because paying for hotel WiFi is highway robbery
  • Bluetooth Hijacking - Your AirPods aren't as secure as you think

When things go sideways:

  • Disconnect immediately - Pull the plug on all networks
  • Change all passwords - Assume everything is compromised
  • Enable 2FA - If you haven't already, what are you waiting for?
  • Monitor accounts - Check for suspicious activity
  • Wipe browser data - Clear cookies, cache, everything
  • Check for malware - Run full system scans
  • Revoke API keys - Rotate all tokens and credentials
  • Contact your bank - If you made any questionable decisions

Remember: Paranoia is a survival skill at DefCon.
